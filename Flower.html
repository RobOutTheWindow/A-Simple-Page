Temples and shrines would tend to use groves and sacred trees as part of their rituals and sacred trees are distinct by being non-fruit bearing forest trees. The sacred tree would be planted between the spring and the altar, few examples being a palm when Odysseus meets Nausicaa and the great oak at Dodona when he hears the voice of the Thunderer. These shrines in groves spread troughout Roman empire and the cult of Isis that reached as far North as the Danube. along the limes.

The cult of Adonis (and plant pots) The cult of Adonis was a offshoot of the cult of Aphrodite around 600 B.C. Adonis was the lover of Aphrodite who was killed by a boar sent by Artemis and Ares jealous o Adonis. The festival of Adonis was performed by women in midsummer where they would plant crops in pottery ontop of their roof, perhaps the origins of plant pots.

Philosophers gardens where the first of a elite group to move away from the public Gymnasiums and baths to set up their own private spaces away from the noise and crowds of the demos. Notably Plato began to teach his pupils in his private garden closeby to the gynasium which he lent to the use of schooling as it was used until the time of Justinian. This became known as the academy and inside its grounds was bulit a Exedra and mueseum. Plato then lived there within the grounds.

Epicurus also had a garden within the city walls which Pliny ascribes to being the first town garden which many Romans would in turn copy to create for themselves pleasure parks within the walls of their cities.
